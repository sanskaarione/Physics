Physics Course Syllabus

Basic & Applied Physics Program

FIRST YEAR: Certificate Course in Basic Physics

Semester I

  • Unit I: Vectors Algebra: Vector algebra, Scalar and vector products, scalar and vector triple products, Derivative of a vector, Del operator, gradient divergence and curl, Gauss divergence theorem, Stokes curl theorem, Green's theorem, Line, surface and volume integral of a vector function.
  • Unit II: Gravitation field and potential: Gravitational field and potential, Gravitational potential energy, Gravitational field Intensity and potential due to various shapes, Gravitational self-energy, Inverse square law, Kepler’s laws.
  • Unit III: Conservation Laws: Frames of reference, Work energy theorem, Conservative forces, Conservation of energy, Centre of mass, Angular momentum, Laws of conservation of energy, linear and angular momentum.
  • Unit IV: Dynamics of rigid body and Moment of Inertia: Translatory and Rotatory motion, Moment of inertia, Theorems of parallel and perpendicular axes, Moment of inertia of various bodies, Kinetic energy of rotation, Compound pendulum.
  • Unit V: Properties of Matter: Elastic constants, Torsion of cylinder, Bending of beam, Viscosity, Stokes’s law, Bernoulli’s theorem, Surface tension.

Semester II

  • Unit I: Electric field and potential: Coulomb law, Gauss’ theory, Electric field and potential, Electrostatic energy, Electric field for various distributions.
  • Unit II: Electric and Magnetic fields in Matter: Polarization, Dielectric susceptibility, Clausius-Mossotti relation, Magnetization, Diamagnetic, paramagnetic, ferromagnetic substances, Hysteresis, Langevin’s theories, Weiss theory.
  • Unit III: Electric Currents (Steady and Varying): Current density, Ohm’s law, Kirchhoff’s Laws, Transient current, Growth and decay in L-R and L-C circuits, Capacitor charging/discharging.
  • Unit IV: Magnetostatics: Lorentz force, Bio-Savert’s law, Ampere’s law, Magnetic field calculations, Magnetic vector potential, Energy in Magnetic field.
  • Unit V: Electromagnetic Induction and Alternating Current: Faraday’s laws, Lenz’s law, Self/Mutual inductance, AC circuits (R-C, R-L, L-C-R), Resonance, Quality factor, Power in A.C. circuits.

SECOND YEAR: Diploma in Applied Physics

Semester III

  • Unit I: Basic concepts and First law: Thermodynamic Systems, Zeroth law, First law, Heat and Work, Adiabatic Processes, Vander Wall equation, Gas expansions.
  • Unit II: Second law and Entropy: Carnot’s Engine, Second law, Thermodynamic scale of temperature, Entropy, T-S diagram, Third law.
  • Unit III: Thermodynamic Relations: Thermodynamic potentials, Maxwell’s equations, Clausius–Clapeyron’s equations, Triple point.
  • Unit IV: Transport of Heat and Kinetic theory: Black body radiation, Stefan Boltzmann Law, Planck’s formula, Kinetic theory of Gases, Law of Equipartition of Energy.
  • Unit V: Fundamentals of Statistical Mechanics: Probability, Ensembles, Phase space, Boltzmann distribution, Classical and Quantum statistics.

Semester IV

  • Unit I: Geometrical Optics: Fermat’s Principle, Cardinal points of an optical system, Thick lens and lens combinations.
  • Unit II: Optical Instruments: Eyepieces (Ramsden’s, Huygen’s), Telescope, Spectrometer, Chromatic and Monochromatic aberrations.
  • Unit III: Interference of Light: Principle of superposition, Two slit interference, Fresnel biprism, Thin films, Haidinger fringes.
  • Unit IV: Diffraction of Light: Fresnel Diffraction (Zone plate, straight edge), Fraunhofer diffraction (Single slit, Double Slit, Grating).
  • Unit V: Polarization of Light: Transverse nature of light, Malus law, Brewster’s law, Nicol prism, Circular and elliptical polarization, Double refraction.
